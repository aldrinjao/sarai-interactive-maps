Suitability Maps

Knowing what and where to plant are important considerations if you want to maximize your productivity and yield. It is also important to identify the parts of the country which are feasible to plant so that the policy makers and the local government unit can be guided on the agricultural programs that they will execute.

The SARAI Suitability Maps can be used to identify the different locations of the country which are highly suitable, moderately suitable, and marginally suitable for its initial six crops. By identifying these areas, resources will not be put to waste and farmers can attain more yield.

How to use

  1. Selecting Crops - Choose the crop you want. You may also choose the suitability level of the selected crop below.

  2. Suitability Level - Check the box of your desired suitability level.

  3. Type in a location to determine its suitability for the selected crop.

  4. Map Source - Select one from the many types of maps to have different views of the selected location.

  1. Zoom in and out of the map to have a detailed view.

  2. Measure the area of a specific location using this tool.

  3. Drag the slider to make the slider lighter or darker.

  4. Translate the page to Filipino. You may also watch tutorial videos on how to use the application.

  5. Share & Download - Share the information to friends and family by sharing the 15-layer map on Facebook or Twitter. You may also download the image for future reference.
